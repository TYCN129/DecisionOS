# DecisionOS Recovery

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

Recovery Mode is the safe operating mode of DecisionOS.

Its purpose is to restore stability after significant disruption.

Recovery Mode does not attempt to maximize productivity.

Recovery Mode maximizes stability.

Stability comes first.

Optimization comes later.

---

# Philosophy

DecisionOS assumes disruption is inevitable.

Examples

- Illness
- Travel
- Burnout
- Emotional stress
- Busy work weeks
- Family emergencies
- Unexpected deadlines

The operating system should not ask:

"How do we prevent disruption forever?"

Instead it asks:

"How quickly can we become stable again?"

Recovery is therefore a permanent feature of DecisionOS.

---

# When Recovery Mode Activates

Recovery Mode activates whenever one or more of the following occur.

Multiple missed days.

Health deteriorates significantly.

Burnout.

Campaign completely stalls.

Major life event.

Extended travel.

Emotional exhaustion.

User explicitly requests Recovery Mode.

---

# Recovery Goals

Recovery Mode has only four goals.

Restore Capacity.

Restore Routine.

Restore Momentum.

Return to Normal Mode.

Nothing else matters.

---

# Rule 1

Forget The Backlog

The backlog does not exist.

Never attempt to "catch up."

Never compensate.

Never schedule double work.

Yesterday is finished.

DecisionOS always starts from today.

---

# Rule 2

Protect Capacity

Before attempting meaningful work,

restore:

Sleep.

Nutrition.

Movement.

Hydration.

Personal hygiene.

Mental recovery.

Capacity always comes before output.

---

# Rule 3

Reduce Complexity

Recovery Mode intentionally simplifies life.

Cancel unnecessary commitments.

Reduce planning.

Reduce decisions.

Reduce cognitive load.

Recovery should feel easier than Normal Mode.

---

# Rule 4

One Artifact

Recovery Mode requires exactly one meaningful artifact.

Examples

Fix one bug.

Complete one interview question.

Ship one small feature.

Publish one post.

Complete one workout.

The objective is momentum.

Not productivity.

---

# Rule 5

Maintenance First

Every recovery day begins with maintenance.

Oracle.

Food.

Sleep.

Exercise.

Relationships.

Maintenance stabilizes the operating system.

---

# Rule 6

No Strategic Decisions

Recovery Mode never changes:

Campaigns.

North Star.

Long-term plans.

Career direction.

Business ideas.

Major purchases.

Recovery Mode exists to restore execution,

not redesign life.

---

# Recovery Checklist

Step 1

Clean workspace.

↓

Step 2

Sleep adequately.

↓

Step 3

Complete maintenance.

↓

Step 4

Create one artifact.

↓

Step 5

End the day.

Do not continue working because momentum returned.

Recovery Mode intentionally ends early.

---

# Recovery Levels

Level 1

Minor Drift

Missed one day.

Solution

Resume tomorrow.

No Recovery Mode required.

---

Level 2

Momentum Loss

Missed multiple days.

Feeling overwhelmed.

Recovery Mode for one day.

---

Level 3

System Instability

Burnout.

Illness.

Major disruption.

Recovery Mode remains active until stability returns.

---

# Recovery Dashboard

While Recovery Mode is active,

the dashboard should display:

Recovery Status

Recovery Day

Current Focus

Capacity

Today's Artifact

Normal metrics become secondary.

---

# Returning To Normal Mode

Recovery Mode ends only when all of the following are true.

Sleep stabilizes.

Maintenance completed consistently.

One artifact created.

Motivation is irrelevant.

Capacity has returned.

Only then should Navigator resume full operation.

---

# Failure During Recovery

Failure inside Recovery Mode is expected.

Do not activate another recovery mode.

Simply restart Recovery Mode tomorrow.

Recovery should never become emotionally expensive.

---

# What Recovery Never Does

Recovery Mode never:

Creates giant to-do lists.

Asks the user to compensate.

Measures streaks.

Measures productivity.

Introduces new goals.

Changes campaigns.

Punishes failure.

Creates guilt.

Recovery exists to remove pressure,

not increase it.

---

# Recovery Success

Recovery succeeds when:

Capacity increases.

Routine returns.

Momentum returns.

Decision fatigue decreases.

Normal operation resumes.

---

# Safe Mode Analogy

Recovery Mode is equivalent to Safe Mode in an operating system.

Only essential services run.

Everything else waits.

Attempting full performance before stability returns increases the probability of future failure.

DecisionOS therefore prioritizes stability over speed.

---

# Final Principle

The objective of Recovery Mode is not to recover yesterday.

The objective is to make tomorrow executable.

Whenever uncertain,

choose the option that makes restarting easier.
