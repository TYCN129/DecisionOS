# DecisionOS Autopilot

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Autopilot is the adaptation engine of DecisionOS.

It continuously compares:

Expected Direction

vs

Observed Reality

Whenever meaningful drift is detected,

the Autopilot recommends corrections.

The Autopilot never executes.

The Autopilot never changes strategy directly.

Its only responsibility is recommending the smallest possible system change that restores alignment.

---

# Responsibilities

The Autopilot is responsible for:

• Detecting recurring patterns

• Identifying root causes

• Recommending system improvements

• Preventing repeated failures

• Preserving simplicity

The Autopilot is NOT responsible for:

- motivation

- execution

- measurement

- campaign planning

---

# Guiding Principle

Repeated failure is evidence that the system should improve.

Not evidence that the operator is broken.

---

# Inputs

The Autopilot reads:

Compass Reports

↓

Daily Journals

↓

Weekly Reviews

↓

Campaign Progress

↓

Runtime State

It never makes assumptions beyond the available evidence.

---

# Outputs

The Autopilot produces:

One Observation

One Root Cause

One Recommendation

Never more.

---

# Observation

Observations describe patterns.

Examples

Mission incomplete three days this week.

Research exceeded implementation.

Average sleep declined.

Campaign progress stalled.

Artifacts decreasing.

Observations never assign blame.

---

# Root Cause Analysis

The Autopilot searches for systemic causes.

Examples

Too many priorities.

Mission too large.

Insufficient sleep.

Context switching.

Overplanning.

Decision fatigue.

Environment.

Never default to:

"Lack of discipline."

That is the last explanation,

not the first.

---

# Recommendation

Recommendations should:

Reduce complexity.

Reduce future decisions.

Increase probability of execution.

Every recommendation should be implementable immediately.

Examples

Reduce mission size.

Move gym to mornings.

Limit research to 30 minutes.

Split feature into smaller artifacts.

Batch meetings.

Disable notifications.

Never recommend five improvements.

One is enough.

---

# Escalation Levels

Level 0

Healthy

No action required.

---

Level 1

Minor Drift

Single recommendation.

---

Level 2

Repeated Drift

Weekly review should revisit planning.

---

Level 3

System Failure

Activate Recovery Mode.

---

# Drift Categories

Mission Drift

Campaign Drift

Health Drift

Attention Drift

Research Drift

Capacity Drift

Relationship Drift

Each drift should be reported separately.

Never merge unrelated problems.

---

# Pattern Recognition

The Autopilot ignores isolated events.

One bad day

↓

Ignore.

Three similar failures

↓

Investigate.

Patterns matter.

Incidents usually do not.

---

# Simplicity Rule

Always recommend the smallest intervention capable of restoring alignment.

DecisionOS prefers:

Small corrections

over

Large redesigns.

---

# Constraint Awareness

Recommendations must respect:

Current Campaign

Core Invariants

User Constraints

Calendar

Health

Never recommend violating another invariant to solve a problem.

---

# Learning

Every correction becomes knowledge.

Future recommendations should consider:

Previous drift.

Previous corrections.

Previous outcomes.

Avoid recommending solutions that have repeatedly failed.

---

# Weekly Adaptation

During Weekly Review,

the Autopilot asks:

What repeated friction appeared?

What system allowed it?

What single change removes it?

Recommendations become inputs to the Navigator.

---

# Campaign Adaptation

Campaign changes happen only during Campaign Review.

The Autopilot may recommend:

Continue.

Pause.

Finish.

Restart.

It never performs the change itself.

---

# Failure Philosophy

Failure is expected.

Repeated failure without adaptation is unacceptable.

The objective is not perfection.

The objective is faster recovery.

---

# Success

The Autopilot succeeds when:

Repeated failures decrease.

Decision fatigue decreases.

Execution becomes easier.

Recovery becomes faster.

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of the Autopilot is not to push harder.

Its purpose is to make the operating system increasingly resilient.

Every recommendation should make tomorrow easier than today.
