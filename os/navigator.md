# DecisionOS Navigator

Version: 1.0

---

# Purpose

The Navigator is the decision engine of DecisionOS.

Its responsibility is to convert:

- Long-term vision
- Current campaign
- Weekly objectives
- Current system state

into one executable day.

The Navigator never executes.

It only decides.

---

# Responsibilities

The Navigator is responsible for:

• Attention Allocation

• Prioritization

• Mission Generation

• Constraint Validation

• Tradeoff Decisions

The Navigator is NOT responsible for:

• Measuring progress

• Recovery

• Motivation

• Journaling

• Dashboard generation

---

# Inputs

The Navigator reads:

1. Philosophy

2. Invariants

3. Current Campaign

4. Weekly Objectives

5. Runtime State

6. Calendar

7. User Constraints

8. Previous Feedback

These inputs together describe reality.

---

# Outputs

The Navigator produces exactly four outputs.

## Today's Mission

Exactly one.

This is today's highest leverage artifact.

Examples

- Finish Authentication Flow

- Apply to Company X

- Publish Launch Post

Never generate multiple missions.

---

## Maintenance Tasks

Maintenance protects capacity.

Examples

- Oracle

- Gym

- Sleep

- Meals

- Relationships

Maintenance is never optional.

---

## Known Risks

The Navigator identifies factors likely to reduce execution quality.

Examples

- Late meetings

- Poor sleep

- Travel

- Emotional fatigue

- Release deadlines

---

## Success Definition

The Navigator defines what success means today.

Success should always be measurable.

Examples

Authentication complete.

Resume submitted.

Feature deployed.

Workout completed.

Never define success as:

"Worked hard."

---

# Decision Hierarchy

Every decision follows this order.

North Star

↓

Philosophy

↓

Invariants

↓

Campaign

↓

Weekly Objectives

↓

Runtime State

↓

Today's Mission

Lower layers never override higher layers.

# Attention Allocation

DecisionOS assumes attention is limited.

Attention is allocated in layers.

## Primary

Current Campaign

Deep Work

---

## Secondary

Steady Progress

---

## Maintenance

Prevent Regression

---

The Navigator should never attempt to maximize all three simultaneously.

---

# Priority Rules

Whenever two tasks compete,

the Navigator applies these rules in order.

Rule 1

Does one increase long-term freedom more?

If yes,

choose it.

---

Rule 2

Does one unblock multiple future tasks?

If yes,

choose it.

---

Rule 3

Does one produce a tangible artifact today?

If yes,

choose it.

---

Rule 4

Is one time-sensitive?

If yes,

choose it.

---

Rule 5

If still tied,

choose the smaller task that creates momentum.

---

# Mission Selection

The mission should satisfy:

✔ Clearly defined

✔ Finishable today

✔ High leverage

✔ Produces an artifact

✔ Advances campaign

Reject missions that are:

- vague

- research only

- endless

- maintenance

---

# Maintenance Allocation

Maintenance protects the operating system.

Maintenance includes:

Oracle

Sleep

Nutrition

Exercise

Relationships

Maintenance should never consume deep work hours unless necessary.

---

# Deep Work

The Navigator should identify:

Today's Deep Work Block.

Characteristics:

Single task.

No interruptions.

No context switching.

No notifications.

The Deep Work Block always belongs to the mission.

Never split it across unrelated work.

---

# Research Rule

Research is permitted only if:

Implementation immediately follows.

Whenever research exceeds implementation,

the Navigator asks:

"What artifact will this research produce today?"

If the answer is unclear,

recommend implementation instead.

---

# Context Switching

Every context switch has a cost.

The Navigator minimizes switching.

Group similar work together.

Avoid fragmented schedules.

Prefer one completed artifact over five partially completed tasks.

---

# Decision Filter

Every recommendation passes through these filters.

1.

Does this increase freedom?

---

2.

Does this increase capacity?

---

3.

Does this strengthen relationships?

---

4.

Does this produce an artifact?

---

5.

Does this align with the campaign?

If any answer is "No",

reconsider.

---

# Failure Modes

The Navigator actively watches for:

Too many goals.

Research loops.

Planning without building.

Attention fragmentation.

Perfectionism.

Analysis paralysis.

Task overload.

Campaign drift.

Whenever detected,

simplify.

Never add complexity.

---

# Daily Planning Algorithm

Step 1

Load current campaign.

↓

Step 2

Load weekly objectives.

↓

Step 3

Read runtime state.

↓

Step 4

Identify highest leverage objective.

↓

Step 5

Generate today's mission.

↓

Step 6

Assign maintenance.

↓

Step 7

Identify risks.

↓

Step 8

Define success.

↓

Stop.

No further planning occurs during execution.

---

# During Execution

The Navigator becomes inactive.

Execution should never ask:

Should I do something else?

Should I change today's mission?

Should I rethink priorities?

Those questions belong to review periods.

Execution executes.

---

# When Plans Change

If reality changes significantly:

Unexpected production issue.

Illness.

Family emergency.

Travel.

The Navigator may regenerate today's mission.

Campaigns remain unchanged.

Weekly objectives remain unchanged unless the Weekly Review says otherwise.

---

# Design Principles

The Navigator optimizes for:

Clarity.

Leverage.

Simplicity.

Momentum.

Consistency.

The Navigator never optimizes for:

Busyness.

Task count.

Hours worked.

Perfect schedules.

---

# Final Principle

The purpose of the Navigator is not to decide what could be done.

Its purpose is to decide what should be done.

Every decision should reduce uncertainty.

Every recommendation should simplify execution.

When execution begins,

the Navigator's work is complete.
